## Acronym Justification / Explanation
As requested by the CRAN submission guidelines, all domain-specific acronyms 
utilized in the Description field have been fully expanded on first use and are 
listed below for verification:
  
* GOBP: Gene Ontology Biological Processes
  * Within Gene Ontology (GO), the Biological Process (BP) ontology describes 
  larger-scale biological goals or programs that are achieved through the 
  coordinated activities of multiple gene products. These are not individual 
  molecular activities, but rather the overall biological objectives that a gene 
  contributes to.
  
* GOCC: Gene Ontology Cellular Component
  * The Gene Ontology (GO) Cellular Component describes the location within a 
  cell where a gene product (such as a protein or RNA) is found when it performs
  its molecular function.
  
* iDEP: Integrated Differential Expression and Pathway analysis
  * A web application that allows users to seamlessly perform differential 
  expression and pathway analysis on their experimental data
  
* KEGG: Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes
  * A database resource for understanding high-level functions and utilities of 
  biological systems, such as cells, organisms, ecosystems and the biosphere 
  from molecular-level information, especially large-scale molecular datasets 
  generated by genome sequencing and other high-throughput experimental 
  technologies.

* RNA: Ribonucleic Acid
  * From Wikipedia: A polymeric molecule that is essential for most biological 
  functions, either by performing the function itself (non-coding RNA) or by 
  forming a template for the production of proteins (messenger RNA).
  
* RNA-Seq: Ribonucleic Acid Sequencing
  * A method of collecting, storing, and analyzing RNA measurements from 
  experimental subjects/samples
